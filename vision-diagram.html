The Playbook

A structured knowledge base of PortSwigger's established legal positions — governing law, liability caps, IP ownership, payment terms, and more. The AI applies these positions; it does not invent them. Every finding cites the precedent it is based on.

02  —  Triage Categories
● Urgent
Needs immediate attention
Contract deviates from established positions in ways that create real legal or commercial risk. Senior review before any signature.
What triggers it
  • Wrong governing law (non-England & Wales)
  • IP assignment to third party
  • Uncapped liability exposure
  • One-sided indemnification clause
  • Perpetual confidentiality with no carve-outs
What the lawyer gets
Flagged clauses with exact quote, established position, and suggested redline ready to paste into the contract.
◐ Review Required
Standard template, some deviations
Broadly standard structure but contains clauses that deviate from agreed positions in ways worth a quick check.
What triggers it
  • Payment terms outside 30-day standard
  • Auto-renewal with short notice window
  • Non-solicitation period beyond 12 months
  • Novel clause with no matching precedent
  • Confidentiality term under 3 years
What the lawyer gets
Summary of deviations, precedent count, recommended action per clause. Typically 2–3 minutes to resolve.
✓ No Action
Boilerplate — matches standard
All clauses checked against established positions. Everything matches. No deviations found. Can be signed off without reading.
What confirms it
  • English law, mutual obligations
  • Standard liability cap (12 months fees)
  • Mutual confidentiality, 3-year term
  • 30-day payment terms
  • Standard carve-outs present
What the lawyer gets
Confirmed position list with precedent counts. Sign-off takes seconds — high confidence, no reading required.

Novel clauses — those with no matching precedent — are flagged separately and escalated to senior review. Once a decision is made, that decision becomes the next precedent.

The principle

"The AI does not invent legal positions. It applies positions your team has already agreed on — retrieved from a structured playbook, cited in every finding. The lawyer stays in control. The AI removes the noise."

05  —  Phased Rollout — Trust Ladder
Phase 1 — Now
AI advises, lawyer reads all
Every contract gets pre-reviewed. Lawyer still reads everything but arrives briefed — knows where to focus and what the AI found before opening the document.
Phase 2 — As trust builds
Lawyer reads flagged only
No-action contracts can be signed off from the queue summary. Lawyer reads the full document only for Urgent and Review Required items.
Phase 3 — Full confidence
Spot checks on approved
High-confidence no-action contracts are processed automatically. Lawyer spot-checks a sample. Focus shifts entirely to the contracts that actually need them.
